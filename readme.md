# Unnamed TTRPG Project

A genre-neutral ttrpg system that prioritizes simplicity, speed, and player agency.

## Motivation

Popular systems are great, but have become bloated with complexity over the years. Character creation presents players with too many choices and results in play that's bogged down by numbers and tedious details. Combat becomes slow and clunky as players need to roll to attack, add modifiers, roll damage, add modifiers, clarify spell effects, keep track of abilities, passive skills, saving throws, etc. This can all be overwhelming for new players and get in the way for ttrpg veterans.

This system seeks solve these problems by reducing the games we love to their most fun elements and removing everything else. The guiding thesis:

    Story and momentum over rules and stats.
    Fun over balance.
    Combat is kinetic.
    Simplify where possible. 
    Character features over classes.
    Flexibility over options.
    Players should not roll for "permission". 
    Mechanics feel implicit. 

## Combat

1. Melee attacks always hit, unless you roll a one for damage.
2. Ranged attacks always hit if you are within the weapon's stated range. If your target is out of range by up to 20 metres, roll a d20, if you rolled higher than the number of metres away you are from the target, the attack hits and you can proceed to roll damage.
    - eg. Imagine your bow has 40 metres of range and your target is 50 metres away, roll a d20. If you roll a 10 or over, you hit your target and can proceed to roll damage.
3. Players get three actions per turn.
    - attack, interaction, movement all constitute an action
    - actions can be saved to be used as reactions out-of-turn. However, actions are reset at the start of your next turn so any unspent actions are wasted.
4. AC acts as a reducer to damage taken. Incoming damage is reduced for every point of AC you have.
    - For example, if you have 3 AC and take 8 points of damage, you only take 5 points of damage.
5. Players determine their turn order.

#### Critical hit
If you roll the highest number on your die, you scored a critical hit, you can roll that same die again and add it to the total damage.

### Leveling up

Each time you level up you:
1. roll a d6 + your Toughness score and add that much additional health to your health pool.
2. If you are level 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, or 18, choose a new character attribute.
3. gain an additional point to assign to an aptitude of your choice.


### Features

_No dedicated classes. Players pick from a set of "Character Features" that define their character's attributes. Gives flexibility for character creation, allowing characters to evolve over time and not be confined to the "box" a class would put them in._

_Note: This is a work in progress, aim for 10-15 features in each category for base system_ 

Examples:

#### Martial
- **Backstab**: surprising an enemy instantly grants your next attack a critical hit.
- **Combat veteran**: expend three actions for a guaranteed critical hit on your next target.
- **Rage**: When below a quarter of your health pool, all melee attacks are critical hits.
- **Ranged weapon specialist**: you gain three metres range with all raged weapons.
- **Vampyrism**: every time you score a critical hit, gain half as much health (rounded down) as damage dealt.


#### Toughness
- **Armour smith**: Your armour is more effective, add a +1 to your AC. At level 7 add +2, at level 13 add +3, at level 18 add +5.
- **Stoneheart**: Add an extra `1d4` to your health upon leveling. At level 10 add `2d4`.
```$note**: do we want to scale features like this?```
- **Last stand**: When knocked below 0 hp in combat, retain consciousness and 1 action on your turn. You are prone and cannot take movement actions, or score critical hits. If your HP drops below the negative of your total health pool, the normal effects of being "downed" apply.


#### Agility
- **Climber**: Your climbing speed matches your movement speed. You have advantage on Finesse rolls related to climbing.
- **Dash**: Expend an action and gain +10 metres movement.
- **Lightfoot**: So long as you're not wearing heavy armour, you can move an extra one metre per action.

#### Arcane
- **Arcane touched**: You can cast spells. Gain three spell slots and learn five spells. This feature is required to unlock the rest of the arcane features.
- **Luck-touched**: You can re-roll any roll once per day.
- **Mana belt**: You gain an additional spell slot. Gain two at level 9, three at level 18.
- **Recast**: Once per day you may re-cast a spell. This does not count against spell slots used.
